National Parks!

Ten Facts About National Parks!

Acadia National Park
Acadia is the oldest park east of the Mississippi River and the first instance where land was donated to the federal government
Arches National Park
There are more than 2,000 documented arches in the national park, some spanning up to 300 ft.
Badlands National Park
The Badlands are home to the largest know cluster of late Eocene and Oligocene mammal fossils
Biscayne National Park
Six shipwrecks over nearly a century have been mapped on Biscayne National Park's Maritime Heritage Trail
Black Canyon of the Gunnison National Park
Sculpted over two million years with the help of the Gunnison River and forces of weather, the Black Canyon is a staggering 48 miles long.
Bryce Canyon National Park
Bryce Canyon has the largest collection of hoodoos-odd shaped pillars of rock-in the world
